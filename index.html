The DPA, CMA and CDPA are three main computer laws that you probably break every day!



The DPA


the DPA is designed to protect personal data stored on computers or in an organised paper filing system. It covers the protection of personal data like bank details, names and adresses and its safety when it is being colected and stored. The act also gives people rights to protect themselves against any misuse of data held about them.

The CMA


the CMA is designed to protect computer users against wilful attacks and theft of information. Offences under the act include hacking, unauthorised access to computer systems and purposefully spreading malicious and damaging software

The CDPA


the CDPA was designed to protect the copyright of individuals and groups. The act protects the ownership of computer software in a similar way that copyright works with printed material. This act protects the software developer's rights and lasts for fifty years after the writer's death. most copywrightec materials have a © symbol on them and most patented things say that they are patented.





In 2018 most of the above laws will become more strict. The new law wil be called GDPR.
